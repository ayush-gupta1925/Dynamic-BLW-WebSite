Engine Parts

Engine Block

Central structure housing cylinders and other engine parts. Built for durability and precision.

Piston

Reciprocating part that moves due to fuel combustion, converting energy into motion.

Crankshaft

Rotating shaft converting piston movement into rotational power for driving wheels.

Cylinder Head

Seals engine block, contains valves and injectors; critical for fuel-air compression.

Turbocharger

Boosts engine efficiency by forcing extra compressed air into combustion chamber.

Fuel Injector

Delivers precise amounts of fuel into the combustion chamber for efficient ignition.

Camshaft

Controls the timing of valve openings and closings in the internal combustion engine.

Connecting Rod

Connects piston to crankshaft, transferring linear motion to rotational motion.

Oil Pump

Circulates lubricating oil to engine components to reduce friction and wear.

Radiator

Dissipates heat from the engine coolant to prevent overheating of the engine.
